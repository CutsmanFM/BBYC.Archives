GabeStah 20-Sep-21 11:30 AM
We had a brief discussion after the raid theorizing the targeting mechanic of the gauntlet-fired cone (i.e. post-60% shatter). I combed through many videos to try to deduce what the cone targets (boss, off-tank, raid member, random location, etc). Turns out, the extra cone targets a random raid member. - Here's the best example clip I could find, which shows a single shadow priest far from the rest of the raid who is dead-center of the gauntlet cone target: https://youtu.be/VTpOQuBu3wM?t=246 - A second timestamp from the same video confirms it, this time with a warlock targeted by cone with nobody else inside: https://youtu.be/VTpOQuBu3wM?t=365 Since the cone targets a random raid member, we must watch the timing if the upcoming set of cones relative to Malevolence applications -- if we ignore that timing crossover, the entire raid will be stacked on the edge and the gauntlet-fired cone will pass directly through us. Here is a clip from our raid where that crossover timing was most apparent: https://www.twitch.tv/videos/1153770228?t=03h40m40s In this instance, bombs were applied ~3 seconds before the cone was fired. The cone targets a random player, but since we're all moving over to the dispel location there's a huge crossover and a few people have no way to get out in time. Most of the time, this will not be a problem because the Grasp cone won't be coming up when Malevolence is applied and we're stacking to dispel. However, we can expect it at least twice per kill. Here are our attempts showing the timings of Grasp (cone) and Malevolence application (dispel/explosion): https://www.warcraftlogs.com/reports/1xRDLJykVdT9Y74G#phase=3&type=summary&hostility=1&source=165&pins=0%24Separate%24%23244F4B%24casts%24-1%240.0.0.Any%24177287.0.0.NPC%24true%240.0.0.Any%24true%24350694%24or%24casts%24-1%240.0.0.Any%24175729.0.0.Boss%24true%240.0.0.Any%24true%24350469%7C350427&view=events&boss=2432&difficulty=5&wipes=1
GabeStah 20-Sep-21 11:42 AM
The crossover moments are anytime Malevolence is cast within ~8-10 seconds of the Grasp (cone). We get that crossover in attempt #6 at 04:01 and attempt #7 at 03:21. In both cases, multiple people get caught by the cone and killed. I think our best tactic to avoid this issue is, while post-60%: - the raid should always be loosely spread within the "inner ring" of the platform. - when a Grasp (cone) cast is expected, raiders should move along the inner ring toward the gauntlet's current position. The nearer to the center of the platform the easier it is to dodge the standard trifecta cones. Standing slightly off-center toward the gauntlet ensures, even in the worst case scenario when a particular individual is targeted by the gauntlet-fired cone, they can side-step to get out, since the width of the gauntlet cone is only about 40% of its maximum at the edge of the inner ring nearest to the gauntlet. - Finally, but most importantly, when Malevolence is applied if the timers show less than 10 seconds to an upcoming Grasp, DO NOT call for the raid to move to the edge for dispel. Instead, keep the raid within the inner ring (again, toward the gauntlet edge), wait for and dodge cones, then move into position toward the nearest safe edge. Even in the worst case, this gives us a minimum of six seconds to get to the edge and dispel before the lowest Malevolence expires naturally. (edited)

Qiggs suggested an alternative tactic for handling gauntlet cones that we could also consider: Have the raid move directly next to the gauntlet just before a Grasp (cone) cast. This trivializes dodging the gauntlet cone since the width is a minimum when next to it. However, this strat is a gamble, since one of the standard trifecta cones could randomly target our position and it would be at the maximum width since we're on the edge. If this happens, someone is likely to get caught and knocked off. (edited)
