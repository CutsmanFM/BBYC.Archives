The biggest difficulty on this one is it is a strict dps check. The 5th charge will wipe us within a few seconds. If we can survive the 20% burn without too much trouble, I'm thinking we will lust on the 4th charge since that should line up with as many offensive cooldowns as possible. Once we get the boss on farm and we get a faster kill time, I might bump lust up to the 3rd charge. One big thing the dps can do to prepare is to take stock of your cooldowns and plan out when you should use them ahead of time. The boss takes 100% increased damage when he collides into a pillar, so using CDs "on cooldown" is probably not the best strategy. We want to line up our offensive CDs with the 100% damage bonus from pillar destruction as much as possible. For example, if your spec has 3 minute CDs (boomkins, frost mages, affliction warlocks etc) you will have 2 casts max no matter what. The best use therefore would be using your CDs on the 1st and 4th charge at 0:58 and 4:25. (edited)

GabeStah 18-Dec-20 11:27 AM
I would take this a step further (no pun intended) and suggest we do away with the "ranged stack" strat entirely, and instead allow ranged to spread out near their respective chain partner around the area. My understanding is we're stacking solely to force Falling Rubble to land in a controlled pattern. While it does typically target players (though not always), forcing a full-ranged stack has a number of drawbacks:
1
11:27
- Significant loss of DPS/HPS output: Since everyone is moving for every single Falling Rubble cast, upwards of 20+ ranged players are losing ~5 seconds of output time every adjustment. In a spread strat, at most only (2) people would need to move per Falling Rubble cast, allowing all other ranged to remain stationary and cast. - Falling Rubble (and Stonequake puddle) damage is more or less trivial: Across all our attempts (prior to "wipe") we had zero Falling Rubble / Stonequake deaths. Even on Heroic taking a direct Falling Rubble hit will do at most 50% HP in damage to a cloth wearer (~16k physical), so even in an emergency situation a player that needs to take the hit can do so if topped off. By contrast, (34) of our pre-wipe deaths were from Shattering Chain , (3) from Charge, (2) from Stomp, and (1) from Fists. The (3) tank deaths on Lith from melee attacks would also likely be prevented using a spread strat since ranged healers would have more stationary HPS uptime. See death logs: https://www.warcraftlogs.com/reports/mRCb3NWLHcrdByTx#boss=2399&difficulty=3&wipes=1&type=deaths&cutoff=8 - Poor use of geometric space: In an ideal world the entire ranged group would stack on a single pixel point at the very edge of an existing Stonequake pool. In reality, this is difficult and time consuming, so the overlap of Falling Rubble effects and the subsequent Stonequake pools are less than ideal. Individual players/chain pairs can be much more precise about their sole positioning, ensuring if they are targeted their Stonequake lands near an existing one. - Heroic: Chain Slam targets a random player and requires most others to move away lest they get pulled toward the boss, which discourages a full-stack strat. - Mythic: Seismic Shift causes eruption damage to all others players within 4 yds, which inherently forces the raid to spread. RE: Chain Slam
I didn't mean to imply only a targeted chain pair should be soaking Chain Slam (though we should certainly consider it as a dynamic option if we start failing DPS checks in higher difficulties). Instead, I was just making the case that -- like pixel stacking and forcing all ranged to move for rubble -- the more players forced to handle Chain Slam the more downtime there is. Generally, spike damage is easier to heal on fewer targets, and 90k physical pre-mitigation when applied to proper targets is not that much to heal up (even in 3-4 seconds). Numerically, the sweet spot seems to be ~8 ranged soaking each Chain Slam, emphasizing those players with high mitigation and/or high HP (i.e. Hunters, Shamans, Boomkins, and Warlocks). For example, those select roles/classes would've been 9 people in our last raid, each taking ~8,000 from Chain Slam post-mitigation. A simple rule of having all mail wearers/boomkin/warlocks soak each Chain Slam and all other ranged move out should work nicely to both mitigate downtime and keep damage levels reasonable.
